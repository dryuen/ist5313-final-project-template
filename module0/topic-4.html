Before George Riedel, a 10th generation glass maker, introduced a new line of wine glasses, people were using one type of glass to drink out of. Through hosting wine tastings he proved that his invention of different shape and sizes of glasses made the wine taste different for the better. When drinking red wines the glasses with a bigger bowl and opening are more desired because of the oxygen it lets into the wine and ethanols out providing a smoother taste. For white wines smaller bowled glasses are best because it helps the wine stay cool and crisp while maintaining the floral notes of the wine.
