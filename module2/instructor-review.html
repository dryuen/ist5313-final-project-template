Let's review graphs!

[ Video ]

Before we begin solving systems of equations let’s review how to graph a linear function. There are two important parts to a linear function, the y-intercept and the slope. In the slope-intercept form y=mx+b, the m is the slope and the b is the y-intercept. So if we have the equation y=-2/3x+4 -⅔ is the slope and 4 is the y-intercept. We start by plotting our y-intercept.

Remember that the y-intercept is plotted on the y-axis, so we draw a point on the y-axis at 4. Then we use our slope to find the next point. When graphing we always refer to our slope as the change in y over the change in x, or in other words the rise over the run. Our slope is negative so that let’s me know that our line will be going down as we look from left to right.

I am going to start at the y-intercept and count down 2 and to the right 3 and plot another point. I can keep going until I run out of graph. I can also find a point going the opposite direction. Let me start again at my y-intercept. This time I am going to move up 2 and left 3 and plot another point. I can also continue this pattern until I run out of graph. Lastly, I need to draw a line through the points. The line I have drawn is the graph for the equation y=-2/3x+4.
