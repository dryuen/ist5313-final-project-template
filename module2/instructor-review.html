Let's review graphs!

[ Video ]

Before we begin solving systems of equations letâ€™s review how to graph a linear function. There are two important parts to a linear function: the y-intercept and the slope. In the slope-intercept form y = mx + b, the m is the slope and the b is the y-intercept. For our equation, y = -2/3x + 4, our slope m is -2 over 3, and our y-intercept b is +4. To begin graphing, we always start with our y-intercept. Remember that the y-intercept is the point on the y-axis, so let's begin by plotting our y-intercept of +4.

Now we use our slope to find the next point. When graphing we always refer to our slope as the change in y over the change in x, or in other words the rise over run. Since our slope is -2 over 3 I know our line is going to be going down from left to right. To find the next point I'm going to start at the y-intercept. I'm going to go down 2 and to the right 3. I can continue to find points by using the same pattern of going down 2 and right 3. I can also go the opposite directions. Starting again at my y-intercept, instead of going down 2 and right 3, I could go up 2 and left 3 and it still creates a point on that same line.

The last thing we need to do is draw a line through all of our points. And now we have the graph for the equation y = -2/3x + 4.
