Example 1: Guided Practice

In this first example, we will show you step-by-step how to use a graph to solve a system of equations.

[ Video ]

In order to solve a system of equations using a graph, we have to plot both lines and find their intersection. Let’s start by graphing the equation y sub1=x+2. We begin by plotting the y-intercept at 2. Remember that the understood coefficient of any variable is 1. So our slope is 1, or as a fraction 1 over 1. This means we need to rise 1 and run 1. We start at the y-intercept and rise 1, run 1, and plot a point. Let’s go again, rise one, run one, plot a point. We can also go in the opposite direction, down 1 and left 1, plot a point. One more time down 1, left one. Now we can draw our line.

Next, we can graph the line of the second equation. The y-intercept is positive 5. Let’s plot our point on the y-axis at positive 5. The slope of this equation is -2 or in other words -2 over 1. Since our slope is negative we need to go down 2 and right 1, plot a point. Again, down 2 and right 1, one more time down 2, right one. Now let’s draw our line. The solution to our system of equation is where the two lines intersect. We can see that the intersect at this point. The x-coordinate is 1 and the y-coordinate is 3. So our solution is the coordinate point (1,3).
