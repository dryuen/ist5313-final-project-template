Final Review. Let's review what we've learned about using elimination to solve systems of equations.

[ Video ]

Let’s revisit Valerie and Sarah’s situation. If Valerie purchases 2 lbs of apples and 3 lbs of oranges and spends a total of $21 her equation would be 2x+3y=21. If Sarah purchases 4 lbs of apples and 2 lbs of oranges and spends a total of $22 her equation would be 4x+2y=22. Let’s use elimination to solve this system of equations. If we multiply the first equation by -2 we get -4x-6y=-42. If we add the two equations the x’s will cancel out and we are left with -4y=-20. To finish solving we can divide both sides by -4 and we get y=5. Let’s plug that in to the first equation. We have 2x+3(5)=21. We multiply first to get 2x+15=21. We subtract 15 from both sides, so 2x=6. Lastly, we divide to get x=3. So if x represent the cost per lb of apples and y represents the cost per lb of oranges, then a lb of apples cost $3 and a lb of oranges cost $5.
