Final Review. Let's review what we've learned about using elimination to solve systems of equations.

[ Video ]

Let’s revisit Valerie and Sarah’s situation. If Valerie purchases 2 lbs of apples and 3 lbs of oranges and spends a total of $21 her equation would be 2x + 3y = 21. If Sarah purchases 4 lbs of apples and 2 lbs of oranges and spends a total of $22 her equation would be 4x + 2y = 22. Let’s use elimination to solve the system of equations.

To solve this system of equations, we can multiply the top equation by -2 to get rid of the x-terms. That would give us -4x - 6y = -42. Bring down the second equation, and now we can add them. -4x + 4x, our x-terms will cancel. -6y + 2y is -4y. -42 + 22 is -20. Now we can divide both sides by -4 and we get y = +5.

We can use our first equation and replace y with 5 and solve for x. First thing we do is multiply the 3 and the 5 to get a positive 15. Subtract 15 from both sides. We get 2x = 6. Last step, divide both sides by 2. We get x = 3.

Our final answer is x = 3 and y = 5. In the context of this situation, that means it's $3.00 per pound of apples and it's $5.00 per pound of oranges.
