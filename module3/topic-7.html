Driving while intoxicated (DWI) means drunk driving, and the state uses your blood alcohol concentration (BAC) to determine whether you're too intoxicated to operate a motor vehicle.

Below are the state's BAC limits: 21 years old or older: 0.08% Commercial drivers: 0.04% Younger than 21 years old: Any detectable amount.

Alcohol can affect you based on the number of drinks you've had, your body weight, and even your gender. Check out the TX blood alcohol percentage charts to learn more.

Additional Drug and Alcohol Crimes
DWI crimes are mostly related to your BAC levels when operating a motor vehicle and certain other circumstances (as you'll see below). Officers though can still arrest you for other alcohol-related crimes involving your vehicle.

It is illegal to have an open container of alcohol in the passenger area of your vehicle if you're driving or parked on a public highway (Texas defines the â€œpassenger area" as the area designed for people to sit in while traveling).

An open container violation results in a maximum $500 fine and a Class C misdemeanor. Though, if you are arrested for DWI and open container, you'll get a Class B misdemeanor and a minimum of 6 days in jail.

For more information on other kinds of DWI laws, check out Title 10, Chapter 49 of the state's penal code.

Understand Your DWI Penalties:

The DWI penalties in Texas are based on factors like license type, age, and other circumstances (such as having other passengers in the vehicle, or tragic events like death).

Common DWI penalties you can expect include:

Fines and surcharges (also tack on court costs and lawyer fees). License suspension or revocation. Community service. Imprisonment (even if you are a first time offender). DWI education and intervention programs. More expensive car insurance, depending on your provider.
