Example 1: Guided Practice

In this first example, we will show you step-by-step how to use elimination to solve a system of equations.

[ Video ]

When you have two equations that are in standard form instead of slope-intercept form, like the ones in this example, it is often easier to solve the system of equations using the elimination method instead of tables or graphing.

To start solving you want to make sure that the two equations are lined up: x over x, y over y, and constant over constant. Our goal is to be able to add the equations straight down and have one of the variables cancel out. For this example, I'm going to have the x's cancel out. In order for the x terms to cancel out, I need both of them to have the same value but opposite signs. Since the second equation already has a positive 2x, if I can make the top equation have a negative 2x then If I add those two terms they will cancel out to be 0. So what I'm going to do is multiply the entire top equation by -2. I'll distribute -2 to all three terms of the equation. I get -2x. -2 times +3 would be -6y, equals. -2 times +11 is -22.

Next I'm going to rewrite the second equation below. Now if I add these two equations straight down, I see that my x's will cancel out because -2 plus +2 is 0. I'm going to mark it out.

Let's add the other terms. -6y plus -4y would be -10y, equals. On the right hand side we have -22 plus -18 which is -40. Now we have a simple equation of -10y equals -40. It just takes one step to solve it. I would divide both sides by -10 to get y by itself. That leaves me with y equals +4.

Now that we know the value of y, we can find the value of x by plugging in the y value into one of the equations. I'm going to substitute 4 in place of y in that first equation and solve for x.

Here I've rewritten the first equation but I've replaced the y with a 4. Now we're ready to solve for x. The first thing you need to do is multiply 3 and 4. That would give me x plus 12 equals 11. There's just one more step to solve for x. Since it's a +12, I will need to subtract 12 from both sides. That leaves me with x equals -1.

To write our final answer we plug in the x value and the y value into a coordinate point. So the solution to this system of equations is (-1,4).
