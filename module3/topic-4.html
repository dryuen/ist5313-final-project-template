Men and women process alcohol differently. Even though a man and a woman are of the same height and weight, they will experience different effects when consuming equal amounts of alcohol.
Through extensive studies, women have shown to be more adversely affected by alcohol abuse. This relevant data can help women know their alcohol consumption level and understand when to reduce accordingly.

Various diseases affect men and women in different ways, so does the related ailments concerning alcohol. The ill-health consequences of alcohol abuse develop faster in women than in men, as decades of studies and research has been conducted on these ailments

In broad general terms, men are able to handle excessive alcohol consumption better than women. Men have more blood volume and their body fat content less than women, as they are primarily larger in build. The enzyme that breaks alcohol down, dehydrogenase, is a higher concentration in men. The dehydrogenase levels are lower in women, they are normally smaller size with more body fat, and they have regular occurrences of hormonal changes. As a result, women respond much faster and with greater intensity after a round of drinks.
